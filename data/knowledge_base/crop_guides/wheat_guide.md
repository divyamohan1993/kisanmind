# Wheat Cultivation Guide — India

## Varieties
- **HD-2967**: Most popular in Punjab/Haryana, high yield (50-55 qtl/hectare)
- **PBW-343**: Early maturing, suitable for late sowing
- **DBW-187**: Heat tolerant, good for terminal heat stress areas

## Growing Conditions
- **Temperature**: 10-25°C optimal. Below 5°C at flowering reduces yield.
- **Soil**: Loamy to clay loam, pH 6.0-7.5
- **Water**: 4-6 irrigations (crown root, tillering, jointing, flowering, milking, dough)
- **Sowing**: November 1-30 (optimal), December 1-15 (late, reduced yield)

## Growth Stages
| Stage | Days After Sowing | NDVI Range |

| Germination | 0-15 | 0.10-0.20 |

| Maturity | 120-140 | 0.25-0.35 |

## Common Problems
- **Yellow rust**: Appears as yellow-orange pustules on leaves, spreads in cool moist weather
- **Lodging**: Stems fall over in wind/rain — risk above 50 km/h wind or heavy rain at grain filling stage
- **Terminal heat**: Above 35°C during grain filling causes shriveled grains

## Harvest Timing
- Harvest at golden-yellow color, grain moisture 12-14%
- Delay of 1 week past maturity can reduce yield 2-3 qtl/hectare
- Rain at harvest causes grain sprouting — harvest immediately if rain forecast
