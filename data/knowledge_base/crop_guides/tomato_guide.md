# Tomato Cultivation Guide — India

## Varieties
- **Pusa Ruby**: Early maturing (60-65 days), suitable for North India plains and hills
- **Arka Rakshak**: Triple disease resistant, high yield (75-80 tonnes/hectare)
- **Arka Samrat**: Heat tolerant, suitable for summer cultivation

## Growing Conditions
- **Temperature**: 18-30°C optimal. Below 10°C causes frost damage. Above 40°C causes flower drop.
- **Soil**: Well-drained loamy soil, pH 6.0-7.0
- **Water**: 500-600mm total, critical during flowering and fruiting
- **Spacing**: 60cm × 45cm

## Common Problems
- **Late blight**: Spreads rapidly in cool, wet conditions (>85% humidity, 15-20°C)
- **Fruit cracking**: Caused by irregular watering or heavy rain after dry spell
- **Blossom end rot**: Calcium deficiency, aggravated by irregular watering

## Harvest Timing
- Harvest when fruits show color break (turning from green to pink)
- Morning harvest preferred (cooler temperatures reduce field heat)
- If rain forecast within 48 hours, harvest all mature fruits immediately
- Handle carefully — tomatoes bruise easily, reducing market value

## Market Information
- Peak season prices typically lower (May-July for hills)
- Off-season (Nov-Feb) commands 2-3x premium
- Shimla and Delhi mandis offer best prices for Himachal growers
- Transport in ventilated crates, avoid direct sun exposure
