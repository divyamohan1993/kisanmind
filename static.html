Problem Statement #5

Indian farmers make ₹45 lakh crore in decisions annually. Blind.

Today's Reality

  • Farmer can't see what satellites see about their crop
  • Sells at nearest mandi, not the most profitable one
  • "Rain expected" doesn't say whether to irrigate, harvest, or spray
  • Advisory only in English/Hindi — useless for 60% of farmers
  • No way to know if soil is dry at root level or just on surface

KisanMind

  • 4 satellites tell crop health, soil moisture, heat stress, root water
  • Ranks mandis by net profit after transport + commission + spoilage
  • Date-specific actions: "Harvest before March 30 rain"
  • Voice-first in 22 Indian languages — just call and speak
  • Cross-validates: "Soil moist but NDVI declining → pest, not drought"

Intelligence Layer

We don't just relay data. We cross-validate it.

When satellites disagree with weather, or prices contradict trends — we catch it before the farmer gets wrong advice.

Signal Sources Action
Crop declining + rain adequate Sentinel-2 vs Weather Pest/disease → KVK (NOT irrigation)
Crop declining + soil confirmed dry Sentinel-2 vs SAR High-confidence: irrigate now
Heat stress + flowering stage MODIS vs GDD model Protect crop (shade/morning water)
Rain coming + harvest-ready Weather vs Growth stage Harvest before rain
Surface wet, roots dry SMAP layers Deep irrigation (rain didn't reach)
Price above 90-day average AgMarkNet history Sell now (may correct down)

Engineering

Built to never fail a farmer mid-call.

5-Model Fallback Gemini 3 Flash → 2.5 → 2.0 → Lite → 1.5 + Vertex AI
3-Tier Cache L0: Satellite grid O(1) · L1: Memory 0.13s · L2: GCS ~200ms
Retry on Rate Limit Extracts API delay, waits (max 15s), retries per model
Graceful Degradation One satellite fails? Others still return. Advisory adapts.
Confidence Gating LOW: omit. MEDIUM: hedge. HIGH: state as advice.
Fact-Check Gemini verifies advisory against raw source data before delivery.
